# **Land Rights and Tenure Systems**

Land rights are fundamental to carbon projects as they determine who has authority to use, control, and make decisions about the land where project activities occur.

## **Types of Land Rights**

Carbon projects operate within complex land tenure systems that often include both customary and formal rights frameworks.

<Step number="1" title="Customary Rights">
* **Customary rights** are traditional, often unwritten, and recognised by communities (e.g., ancestral land, communal grazing areas)
</Step>

<Step number="2" title="Formal Rights">
* **Formal rights** are granted or recognised by the state (e.g., land titles, leases)
</Step>

<Callout type="info">
Some projects operate entirely on **customary land**; others on **state-owned or titled land** — and some on a mix.
</Callout>

## **What to Document in Your Project**

<Callout type="tip" title="You Should Record">
* Who holds each type of right (individual, family, group, government)
* Any overlaps or unresolved claims
</Callout>

## Why Land Rights Matter

<Callout type="info" title="Why It Matters">
Acknowledging both systems supports fairness, avoids conflict, and helps meet legal and certification requirements.
</Callout>
