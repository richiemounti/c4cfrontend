# **What is Project Governance?**

Governance describes **who has power, permission, and responsibility** in your project. This includes:

## Key Governance Actors to Document

<Step number="1" title="Those who approved the project">
- Examples include: village assembly, district council, government agency
</Step>

<Step number="2" title="Those who implement the project day-to-day">
- Examples include: NGOs, private developers, community groups
</Step>

<Step number="3" title="Those who oversee it">
- Examples include: wildlife authorities, land commissions, registries
</Step>

<Callout type="info">
You should include **both formal actors** (with legal mandates) and **customary or informal authorities** (e.g. elders, clan councils).
</Callout>

## Why Governance Matters

<Callout type="tip" title="Why It Matters">
Documenting governance helps clarify decision-making, manage expectations, and ensure accountability across actors.
</Callout>
